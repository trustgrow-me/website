TrustGrow and the general data protection regulation (GDPR)

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is an EU privacy framework designed to give individuals more control over their personal data. It applies globally to any business processing the data of EU residents — regardless of where that business is located.

This page outlines how TrustGrow applies GDPR principles and ensures that personal data is collected, processed, and stored transparently and securely.

Purpose of this document

TrustGrow is committed to full transparency around data protection. This page gives you a clear overview of:

  • Who we are
  • What types of data we collect
  • Why we collect data
  • How long we retain your data
  • Third-party services we work with
  • Your rights over your data
  • How you can manage, update, or delete your information
  • How to contact us regarding privacy questions

What is GDPR?

GDPR gives individuals full control over how companies use their personal information. Businesses like TrustGrow must clearly explain what data is collected, why it is collected, and how long it is retained. Users have the right to access, correct, restrict, or delete their data at any time.

GDPR applies to TrustGrow because we serve users and clients located within the European Union.
